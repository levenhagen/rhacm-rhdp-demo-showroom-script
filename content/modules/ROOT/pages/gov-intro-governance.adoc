== Concepts & Architecture

After deploying and importing managed clusters, and as part of its _day-2 configurations_, clusters need policies to ensure configurations are properly applied for receiving workloads and user access. 

More importantly, policies will make sure the clusters do not drift from their original desired state. 

This kind of drift is a serious problem, because it can happen from beginning and benevolent fixes and changes, as well as malicious activities that you might not notice but can cause significant problems. 
Here's an diagram to explain the governance architecture:

image::governance-arch.png[RHACM Governance architecture]
In short, the RHACM Hub cluster has a _Policy controller_. Policy controllers monitor and report whether the managed clusters are compliant with the resources defined by a policy, or not.

== Policy engines

Also, multiple controllers (or _policy engines_) might be available, each one for different purposes. In the illustration above, you might have noticed a few different ones: RHACM supports its own policies (https://open-cluster-management.io/docs/getting-started/integration/policy-controllers/[Open Cluster Management] policies), as well as Gatekeeper policies, https://kyverno.io/[Kyverno] policies and Kubernetes https://kubernetes.io/docs/reference/access-authn-authz/validating-admission-policy/[ValidatingAdmissionPolicies].

image::governance-supported-policies.png[Supported Policy engines/controllers]

In this lab, we will focus only on RHACM policies. Gatekeeper, Kyverno and ValidatingAdmissionPolicies are not covered as part of this course.

== Policy types

Ultimately, it is important to define *Policy types*. Policy is a very powerful capability and different policies can achieve different things.
Policy types are described below, but they ultimately serve to govern the configurations of your clusters. 

The following table describes the three types of policy controllers available in RHACM, along with its remediation mode:

[cols="1,3,1",options="header"]
|===
|Policy type |Purpose |Remediation mode 

|Certificate Policy
|Used to detect certificates that are close to expiring. You can configure the certificate policy controller by updating the minimum duration parameter in your controller policy. When a certificate expires in less than the minimum duration, the policy becomes noncompliant. Certificates are identified from secrets in the included namespaces.
|Inform

|Configuration Policy
|Used to configure any Kubernetes resource across your clusters. Where these resources are created or configured is determined by the namespaces you include (or exclude) in the policy.
|Inform & Enforce

|Operator Policy
|OperatorPolicy is a specialized feature in RHACM (2.11+) designed to manage the lifecycle of Kubernetes-native operators and their applications across multiple clusters. It streamlines installing, configuring, upgrading, and removing operators, featuring enhanced control over initial installations, alert management, and automated remediation. It provides a better integration with OLM than ConfigurationPolicies.
|Inform & Enforce

|===
