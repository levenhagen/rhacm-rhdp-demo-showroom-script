= Business Continuity
:navtitle: Business Continuity

Business Continuity represents a set of processes dedicated to maintaining the continuity of your cluster services and ensuring they remain available during disruptions. In the context of cloud-native technologies, it encompasses the strategies, architectures, and operational practices required to minimize downtime, protect critical workloads, and enable rapid recovery from failures.

== Concepts & Architecture

First off, in the context of RHACM, we can talk about two different aspects of Disaster Recovery mechanisms:

* 1: Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery for Applications.
* 2: Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery for the RHACM Hub itself.

Let's focus on *#1* first:

RHACM teams up with Red Hat OpenShift Data Foundation to provide resiliency for applications. 
In this lab, we won't focus deeply on DR and simulate a disaster, but we'll explain what it looks like.

=== Draw me a picture!

RHACM & OpenShift Data Foundation together provides *Regional Disaster Recovery (Regional-DR)* and *Metro Disaster Recovery (Metro-DR)*.

* *Regional-DR* is a cloud computing strategy that backs up applications and data across different geographic areas. If a primary datacenter goes offline, workloads safely switch to a secondary region. This ensures systems stay online during large-scale outages.

image::bc-regional-dr.png[Regional-DR]

* *Metro-DR* capability provides volume persistent data and metadata replication across sites that are in the same geographical area. In the public cloud these would be similar to protecting from an Availability Zone failure. Metro-DR ensures business continuity during the unavailability of a data center with no data loss. This is usually expressed at Recovery Point Objective (RPO) and Recovery Time Objective (RTO).

image::bc-metro-dr.png[Metro-DR]

NOTE: **RPO vs RTO**: **RPO** is a measure of how frequently you take backups or snapshots of persistent data. In practice, the RPO indicates the amount of data that will be lost or need to be reentered after an outage. Metro-DR solution ensures your RPO is zero because data is replicated in a synchronous fashion. 
*RTO* is the amount of downtime a business can tolerate. The RTO answers the question, “How long can it take for our system to recover after we were notified of a business disruption?”

image::bc-comparison-dr.png[Backup vs Regional-DR vs Metro-DR]
